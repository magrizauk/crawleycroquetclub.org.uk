About the Club

A Club for Everyone, in the Heart of Sussex

We play at the gorgeous Worth Park in Crawley, and our season runs from mid-April through to the end of September. Club days are four relaxed afternoons a week — come in whatever you're wearing, bring a friend, and enjoy the fresh air. You don't need to be fit, competitive, or retired to enjoy croquet; our members range widely in age, background, and ability.

We play Golf Croquet — the faster, more accessible form of the game that's easy to pick up on your first visit. Unlike Association Croquet, there's no need to learn complex break play; the tactics are straightforward enough to enjoy from day one, yet deep enough to keep you engaged for years.

New to croquet? Come along for a free introductory lesson and your first three games completely free, no strings attached. Just give us a quick call or email so we can arrange a warm welcome.

The only thing we ask is that you wear flat-soled shoes — trainers are perfect. This protects the lawns and keeps everyone safe. We provide all the equipment you need.

Reasons to Play

Why Croquet?

Beautiful Setting

Our lawns at Worth Park are set within stunning Victorian parkland — a genuinely lovely place to spend a summer afternoon outdoors.

Wonderfully Social

Croquet is a game of tactics, conversation, and good humour. Our members come from all walks of life and all parts of Crawley — the only thing they have in common is that they love the game and the company.

Surprisingly Strategic

Behind the leisurely pace lies a genuinely tactical game. Easy to pick up, endlessly satisfying to master — and it keeps the mind sharp.

Come As You Are

Forget the stereotype. Club days are relaxed and informal — come in whatever you’re comfortable in. Whites are only worn in competitions, and even then only if you choose to enter one.

Fresh Air & Exercise

A wonderful low-impact way to stay active through the summer months — gentle on the joints, great for wellbeing.

Free First Visit

Not sure if it’s for you? Come along for a free introductory lesson and your first three games on us — no experience, equipment, or special clothing needed. There’s absolutely no obligation, just a warm welcome.

Bring Your Group

Looking for a team day, summer party, or social occasion with a difference? Our lawns at Worth Park offer a unique and memorable setting for private events of all kinds — corporate groups, clubs, and social gatherings all welcome.

Our History

A Century on the Lawns

The Crawley Croquet Club plays in the grounds of Worth Park, a beautifully restored Victorian garden in Pound Hill, Crawley – and our lawns have been part of this landscape for well over a century.

The croquet lawn was laid out as part of a major remodelling of the estate in the 1880s, overseen by the renowned garden builders James Pulham & Son – the same firm responsible for features at Buckingham Palace and Sandringham House.

The lawn sat at the ornamental heart of the estate, framed by Pulhamite balustrades and stone vases, and forming part of a leisure circuit that still connects the formal pond, the lawns, and the tennis court today.

The estate passed through private and school use before Crawley Borough Council took ownership in 1963, and the lawns have remained in continuous use ever since. A major Heritage Lottery Fund restoration – completed in the 2010s – returned the gardens to something close to their Victorian character, and the croquet area was formally recognised as one of the park’s seven distinct character zones.

The club has since taken a further step in securing the future of the lawns – leasing the land directly from the council and taking on responsibility for their maintenance. Today the club manages two full-size lawns, which can be configured as four half-size courts and a practice lawn, all maintained to a high standard by our members within one of West Sussex’s finest public gardens.
